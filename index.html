From Pathologies to Possibilities

Built on research. Designed for thinking. Made for the future.

In his influential 1999 work, Lee Shulman introduced the idea of “pathologies of learning”:

  • Amnesia — forgetting what was taught
  • Fantasia — misunderstanding key concepts
  • Inertia — failing to apply knowledge

These ideas shaped early educational models that aimed to remove barriers to learning.

But learning has evolved — and so have we.

At Codekey™, we don’t just treat learning problems. We build thinking systems that prepare students to reason, reflect, and adapt — for life.

The Codekey™ Difference

Amnesia → Memory failure✅ Spaced Retrieval, Dual Coding, Active Recall
Fantasia → Misunderstanding✅ Schema Activation, Visual Mapping, Concept Repair
Inertia → Lack of Application✅ Transfer Fluency Training, Authentic Problem Solving
“Barriers to Learning” framing✅ Learning Engine Design: Habits, feedback, metacognition
Passive reviews, drills✅ Interleaving, Diagnostic Loops, Thinking Routines
“Shulman helped us understand what can go wrong. At Codekey™, we focus on what makes learning truly work — and stick.”

Codekey™ draws on over two decades of learning science, including works by Ambrose et al. (2010), Bransford et al. (2000), Willingham (2009), and Hattie (2009), to build cognitive fluency and academic resilience in every learner.
